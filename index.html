02 Introduction
The Client

Ember & Oak is an independent fine dining restaurant based in Dublin, Ireland, specialising in seasonal Irish cuisine with a strong focus on locally sourced ingredients. Founded in 2019 by head chef Ronan Byrne, the restaurant quickly built a loyal local following through word of mouth and earned a strong reputation for quality and atmosphere.

Despite consistently positive reviews on Google and TripAdvisor, Ember & Oak was struggling to convert online interest into actual reservations. Potential customers were finding the restaurant, landing on the website, and leaving without booking.

When Ronan approached Sky High Designs, the problem was clear — the restaurant's existing website did not reflect the quality of the dining experience it was selling. Ronan came to SHD looking for a complete redesign that matched the quality of the food, made booking effortless, and gave Ember & Oak the online presence it deserved.

03 The Situation Before SHD
The Before

Before engaging Sky High Designs, Ember & Oak's website was built on a basic Wix template that had not been updated since 2020. The site loaded in over 9 seconds on mobile — a critical problem given that more than 70% of restaurant searches happen on mobile devices.

The menu was a PDF download, the reservation system was a broken third-party widget, and the photography was taken on a phone camera. From an SEO perspective the site had no meaningful structure — no meta descriptions, no local SEO, and no Google Business Profile connection. Analytics showed a bounce rate of 84% and an average session duration under 30 seconds.

Key Problems Identified

  • Website load speed: 9.2 seconds on mobile — benchmark is under 3 seconds
  • Bounce rate of 84% — visitors leaving without any interaction
  • No functional online reservation system — broken third party widget
  • Menu only accessible as a downloadable PDF — not mobile friendly
  • Photography did not reflect the quality of the dining experience
  • Zero local SEO presence — not appearing in Google local pack results
  • No mobile optimisation — site was built for desktop only
  • Google Business Profile disconnected from the website

04 Methodology
The Approach

Sky High Designs approached the project with a mobile-first redesign strategy built around one core objective — reducing the number of steps between a visitor landing on the site and completing a reservation. Every decision was made with this conversion goal in mind.

Rather than simply refreshing the visual design, SHD conducted a full audit before touching a single element — a technical performance audit, a content audit, a competitor analysis of five competing Dublin restaurants, and a review of Ember & Oak's Google Analytics data.

The agreed scope of work covered a full rebuild on WordPress with a custom theme, integration of a reliable reservation system, a mobile-optimised menu, professional photography direction, local SEO foundations, and Google Business Profile optimisation across a twelve week timeline with clear milestones at weeks four, eight, and twelve.

SHD also agreed with the client that the new site would be tested with real users before launch — a group of loyal customers were invited to navigate the site and complete a reservation, with their feedback incorporated before the go-live date.

Focus Area
What Was Done
What Was Kept
The brand name, colour palette, and core content around the restaurant's story and values were retained. The existing logo was preserved with minor refinements. Positive customer review quotes were carried over and featured more prominently on the new site.
What Was Changed
The entire site architecture was rebuilt from scratch. The Wix template was abandoned in favour of a custom WordPress build. Navigation was simplified from seven items to four. The reservation CTA was placed above the fold on every page. The PDF menu was replaced with a fully responsive inline menu with dietary filtering.
Fixing The Problems
Page speed was reduced from 9.2 seconds to 1.8 seconds through image compression, lazy loading, and hosting migration. A new reservation system — Resy — was integrated directly. Professional photography was directed by SHD with guidance on styling, lighting, and shot list.
Migration
All existing content was audited before migration. Blog posts and press mentions were redirected properly to avoid broken links. The old Wix site remained live until the new site passed all QA checks. DNS migration was handled overnight to minimise downtime.
Testing & QA
The site was tested across 12 devices and 4 browsers before launch. A soft launch was conducted with loyal customers who completed a reservation and provided feedback. Two UX issues identified during soft launch were resolved before the public launch date.
06 Results
The Numbers

The results following launch exceeded the targets set at the start of the project. Within the first 90 days, online reservations increased by 312% compared to the same period in the previous year. The bounce rate dropped from 84% to 31%, and average session duration increased from 28 seconds to 3 minutes and 47 seconds.

Ember & Oak moved from page 4 to position 3 on page 1 of Google for 'fine dining Dublin' within six weeks of launch — driven by the local SEO foundations and Google Business Profile optimisation. Direct traffic from Google Maps increased by 218% in the same period.

Ronan reported that the kitchen was fully booked every Friday and Saturday evening for the first time since the restaurant opened — something he attributed directly to the ease of the new reservation system and the quality of the photography in converting browsers into bookers.

08 Lessons Learned
01

Fix the foundation before the design

The visual redesign is rarely the most impactful part of the project. In this case the single biggest driver of improved results was reducing page load speed from 9.2 to 1.8 seconds. Visitors who would have bounced immediately were now staying long enough to book. Always fix the technical foundations first.

02

User test before launch — every time

The soft launch with a real user testing group identified two UX friction points that would have cost bookings post-launch. Minor fixes in testing prevent major problems in production. Building a user test into every redesign project is now standard SHD practice.

03

Take more ownership of photography direction

Photography was outsourced to a third party on this project with SHD providing only a brief. On future hospitality projects SHD will take a more hands-on role to ensure visual output is fully aligned with the design direction from day one — not adapted around photography after the fact.
